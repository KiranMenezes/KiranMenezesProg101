List of Threats

  • Fragmentation
  • Habitat Loss

  • Poaching

Fragmentation

As the Giant Pandas habitat continues to shrink, and the forests they live in become separated from one another, the groups of Pandas living in those forests are virtually trapped. This results in inbreeding within the Panda population inhabiting a certain area. Inbreeding can have devasting impacts on a species numbers in little time. The decline in numbers concerning an inbreeding population of Pandas is usually attributed to deaths from illness and disease to birth defects that are fatal.



Habitat Loss

Much of the Giant Pandas habitat has been lost due to logging for timber and wood fuel, or clearcut and utilized for agriculture and infrustructure to match the needs of the areas booming population. In 1998, the Chinese Government banned logging in the Panda Habitat. Saldy however, new highways and railroads that are being built are cutting through the region, which is fragmenting the forests. This further isolates panda populations and prevents them from breeding.


Hunting

Hunting and has had an impact on Giant Panda numbers. These fluffly bears were hunted for their fur and their numbers decreased gradually throughout the past hundred years. But due to strict laws and an increase in public awarness the population numbers have began to stabilize.


Poaching

Another threat to the Giant Pandas is poaching. Giant Pandas are an unusual target for poachers, these fluffy animals sadly get accidently injured or trapped in snares set by the poachers for game animals; such as musk deer or black bears.
