Threats to the Red Panda

The main threat to the Red Panda populations around the world are huamns.

List of Threats

  1. Poaching
  2. Deforestation
  3. Competition with domesticated animals
  4. Pollution

Poaching

Illegal hunting of the Red Panda has had a severe impact on the species wild population. Due to the demand for the animals pelt, poaching incidence increased and the Panda population decreased.


Deforestation

Deforesation has created the most problems for the wild Red Pandas. With large sections of forrest gone, the populations of Red Pandas have been cut off from each other. This type of "isolation" results in potential inbreeding. Inbreeding can have many effects on the Red Panda population, deriving from birth defects which can contribute to illness, bodily failure, and more unpredictable results.


Competition with Domesticated Animals

As the development of china continued, people who owned livestock had to move further out into the wilderness to graze their animals. Bringing the livestock further away from civilization meant that the farmers grazing locations would begin to encrouch on the Pandas habitat. Even though the livestock and the wild Pandas aren't in Competition for the same resources, the disruption caused to the land by the livestock has driven the Pandas into small areas that are more remote.


Pollution

Another big threat to the Red Pandas is the pollution or waste generated by humans. The most common types of pollution, which are water pollution, air pollution, and garbage waste have had great impacts on the Red Pandas around the world. The chemicals from these pollutants will drive the animals away from areas they once lived in because of the contamination. As human civilizations grow, the Red Panda population has shrunk in size due to the growing amount of "trash" left in their home.
